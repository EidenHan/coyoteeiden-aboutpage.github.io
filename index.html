About Coyote





Coyotes (Canis latrans) are highly adaptable wild canines native to North America. Traditionally found in deserts and prairies, they‚Äôve expanded into urban and suburban environments due to habitat loss and food availability. Coyotes are about the size of a medium dog, with grayish-brown fur, a bushy tail, and a pointed snout. They are usually most active during dawn and dusk (crepuscular), but in human-populated areas, they may become more nocturnal to avoid people.

Coyotes are opportunistic omnivores. Their diet includes:
  • Small mammals (rabbits, rodents)
  • Fruits and berries
  • Insects
  • Pet food and garbage (in urban areas)
  • Occasionally, small pets like cats or small dogs
